Honey-Mustard Pork Chops with Crushed Baby Potatoes & Buttered Peas

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Serves 5 ⏱ 40 min 🌡 Pork 63°C
No meal plan found for tonight — original recipe using the 4 pork chops in the freezer (not recent in rotation) plus baby potatoes and frozen peas on hand.

Ingredients

  • 4 pork chops (bone-in, ~200 g each) — defrost in fridge or cold water
  • 600 g baby potatoes
  • 300 g frozen peas
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp wholegrain mustard (or Dijon)
  • 1 tbsp cider vinegar (or white wine vinegar)
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves (use the bunch in the fridge)
  • 30 g butter + 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, black pepper

Prep Ahead (before 5:00 PM pickup)

  • ☐ Take pork chops out to defrost/come to room temp (move to fridge in the morning)
  • ☐ Mix the glaze: honey + mustard + vinegar + garlic + thyme — whisk in a small bowl
  • ☐ Wash baby potatoes, halve any large ones
  • ☐ Pat chops dry, season both sides with salt and pepper
🔪 Wash board and knife after handling raw pork before touching anything else.

Timing Plan

4:40 PMPrep glaze, potatoes, season chops (before school run)
5:30 PMPut potatoes on to boil (salted water, ~18 min)
5:40 PMHeat heavy pan — sear chops 3 min each side
5:46 PMPour in glaze, spoon over chops, reduce 3–4 min until sticky. Probe to 63°C.
5:50 PMRest chops 5 min. Drain spuds, crush lightly with butter & salt.
5:53 PMPeas into boiling water 3 min, drain, toss with a knob of butter.
6:00 PMPlate up.

Method

  1. Bring a pan of salted water to the boil. Add baby potatoes and simmer 15–18 min until a knife slides through easily.
  2. Meanwhile, heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a heavy frying pan over medium-high heat. Sear the seasoned pork chops for 3 minutes each side until well browned.
  3. Reduce the heat to medium. Pour in the honey-mustard glaze and let it bubble. Spoon over the chops for 3–4 minutes until thickened and sticky. Probe the thickest chop — pull at 63°C internal.
  4. Lift the chops onto a warm plate to rest for 5 minutes (they'll carry on to ~65°C).
  5. Drain the potatoes, return to the pan, add 20 g butter and a pinch of salt. Crush each lightly with a fork.
  6. Boil the peas for 3 minutes, drain, toss with the remaining butter.
  7. Plate chops, spoon any pan glaze over the top, serve with crushed potatoes and peas.
👶 For the younger two: the glaze is sweet, not spicy, so it's kid-friendly. Slice their chop off the bone and cut into strips. Serve the glaze on the side if they prefer plain.
