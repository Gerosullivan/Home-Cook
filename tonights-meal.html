🥘 Next Level Paella

~1 hr 30 mins total
Serves 5
Seafood · Spanish

Ingredients

Scaled to 5 servings (recipe is 4)

  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • ~400g large raw tiger prawns in shells
  • 1 good bunch of parsley, stalks & leaves separated
  • 125ml dry sherry or white wine
  • 625g mussels, cleaned & debearded
  • Large pinch of saffron strands
  • 190g cooking chorizo, cut into chunks
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • ~375g medium squid, cleaned, cut into rings
  • 4 ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 310g paella rice
  • 125g frozen broad beans or peas, defrosted
  • 1 lemon, zested then cut into wedges
  • Smoked sea salt (optional)

Method

1
Make the prawn head stock ~15 mins (before pickup)
Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wide, shallow pan. Add the prawn heads and parsley stalks, sizzle until the heads turn pink, then mash with a potato masher. Pour over the sherry or wine and 375ml water, season with salt and simmer for 10 minutes, mashing the prawn heads as they cook.
2
Cook the mussels & make saffron stock ~5 mins (before pickup)
Scatter the mussels into the pan, cover loosely with a lid or tea towel, put over high heat for 3–4 minutes until the mussels just open. Stir to release mussel juices, then pour contents into a colander set over a large bowl containing the saffron. Let the saffron steep in the stock — you need about 875ml total (scaled for 5), top up with water if needed. Pick the mussels out and set aside in the fridge.
3
Chorizo, soffritto & rice ~10 mins (after pickup)
Wipe out the pan and add the rest of the olive oil. Sizzle the chorizo until it releases its oil, then add the onion and garlic and cook until softened. Add the squid and turn until it turns white. Add the tomatoes and cook for a few minutes until pulpy. Tip in the rice and stir until coated, then pour in the saffron stock. Bring to a simmer.
💡 Tip: Once the stock is in, resist stirring too much — you want a slight crust (socarrat) to form on the bottom. Stir gently a couple of times early on, then leave it alone.
4
Simmer the rice ~15 mins
Cook uncovered for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally at first then leaving it alone. The stock should be almost absorbed and the rice almost cooked.
5
Add prawns, beans & mussels ~5 mins
Tuck the prawn tails into the rice and simmer for 5 minutes, turning them over until cooked through. Stir through the beans or peas to heat through, and nestle the mussels into the rice to warm.
6
Rest & serve
Once the rice is just cooked, turn off the heat and cover with a tea towel for a few minutes. Scatter over the parsley leaves, lemon zest, and smoked sea salt if using. Serve with lemon wedges.
👧 Kid-friendly note: This paella is mild — the chorizo adds warmth but not heat. Serve the kids' portions from the centre of the pan where the rice is softest. Let any mussels cool a moment — they hold heat. Show the kids how to eat mussels from the shell!
Leftovers: Paella reheats well — splash in a little water, cover with foil, and warm in the oven at 160°C for 15 mins. Don't microwave the mussels though — pick them out and eat cold or discard.
