Pan-Fried Pork Chops with Crushed Baby Potatoes & Honey-Thyme Carrots

~45 mins total
Serves 5
Pork · Irish Comfort
Saturday suggestion: It's takeaway night, but you've got pork chops in the freezer and baby potatoes, carrots & thyme all needing to be used. This is a quick, satisfying dinner if you fancy cooking instead — skip it if you'd rather order in!

Ingredients

  • 4 pork chops (from freezer — defrost in fridge or microwave)
  • 1 bag baby potatoes (~500g)
  • 4–5 carrots, peeled & halved lengthways
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 bunch fresh thyme
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 40g butter
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • Salt & black pepper

Prep Ahead

~25 min prep

Method

1
Boil the potatoes 15 mins
Put baby potatoes in a pot of salted boiling water. Cook for 15 minutes until a knife slides through easily. Drain and set aside.
2
Prep & roast the carrots 5 mins prep + 30 mins oven
Preheat oven to 200°C. Peel carrots, halve lengthways, and toss on a baking tray with 1 tbsp olive oil, honey, red onion wedges, and a few sprigs of thyme. Season well. Roast for 30 minutes until caramelised and tender.
Start the oven before you leave for pickup — the carrots will be perfectly roasted by the time you're back at 5:30 PM.
3
Season the chops 2 mins
Pat pork chops dry with kitchen paper. Season generously on both sides with salt and pepper.
4
Pan-fry the pork chops 8–10 mins
Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add chops and cook 4–5 minutes per side until golden brown with a good crust. In the last minute, add 20g butter, crushed garlic, and a few sprigs of thyme. Baste the chops with the foaming butter.
Thermometer check: Insert a wireless probe into the thickest part of a chop — you're looking for 68°C internal. Rest the chops for 3–4 minutes once they hit temp.
5
Crush the potatoes 5 mins
While chops rest, add 20g butter to the same pan (keep all those lovely pork juices). Tip in the boiled baby potatoes and press each one lightly with a fork to crush and open up. Fry for 3–4 minutes until golden and crispy on the cut sides. Squeeze over lemon juice, scatter with stripped thyme leaves, and toss.
6
Plate up
Serve each plate with a pork chop, a pile of crushed potatoes, and the honey-roasted carrots & onion alongside. Spoon any pan juices over the chops.
Kid-friendly note: This one's a winner for the kids — no heat or spice. The honey carrots go down well. For the younger two, cut the pork off the bone into strips for easier eating.
