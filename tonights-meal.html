Pan-Seared Pork Chops with Honey-Mustard Butter, Creamed Savoy Cabbage & Potatoes

~50 mins total
Serves 5
Pork

Ingredients

  • Pork Chops
  • 500g pork chops (bone-in or boneless)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked or sweet paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme or rosemary
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 20g butter
  • 1 tbsp Dijon or wholegrain mustard
  • 1 tsp honey
  • Roast Potatoes
  • 800g–1kg potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Optional: rosemary or thyme sprigs
  • Creamed Savoy Cabbage
  • 300–400g shredded savoy cabbage
  • 20g butter
  • 100ml cream
  • Optional: pinch of nutmeg

Method

1
Prep & roast the potatoes 10 min prep + 35–40 min roast
Preheat oven to 220°C (fan 200°C). Parboil potatoes in well-salted water for 8–10 minutes until just tender at the edges. Drain and shake in the pot to rough up the edges. Toss with olive oil, salt, and herbs if using. Roast for 35–40 minutes, turning once, until golden and crisp.
Tip: Get the potatoes into the oven before pickup. They'll be nearly done when you're back — just need a final turn and 10 more minutes for extra crispiness.
2
Season the pork 3 mins
Mix paprika, garlic powder, thyme, salt and pepper. Rub over the pork chops with olive oil. Let sit at room temperature while potatoes roast.
3
Sear the pork chops 8–10 mins
Heat a wide frying pan over medium-high heat. Sear pork chops for 2–3 minutes per side until nicely golden. Lower heat slightly, add butter, and baste the chops for another 1–2 minutes per side until just cooked through. Remove to a warm plate and rest for 5 minutes.
Thermometer check: If you want to probe, pork chops are done at 63°C internal — they'll carry over to 65–68°C while resting. No pink fear — they'll be juicy.
4
Make the honey-mustard butter 2 mins
With the pan off the heat, stir mustard and honey into the buttery pork juices. Add a small squeeze of lemon if using. Spoon over the rested pork chops.
5
Creamed savoy cabbage 6 mins
Steam or boil the cabbage for 3–4 minutes until tender. Drain very well. Return to pan with butter and cream. Warm gently for 1–2 minutes. Season with salt and optional nutmeg.
Kid-friendly note: The honey-mustard butter is mild and sweet — kids usually love it. If anyone's not keen on cabbage, serve theirs with just the potatoes and a little butter on the side.
6
Plate up
Plate pork chops with crispy roast potatoes and creamed savoy cabbage. Spoon the honey-mustard butter over the pork. Optional extras on the table: apple sauce, gravy, or extra mustard.
