Pork & Fennel Meatball Orzo

~45 mins total
Serves 5
Pork · Italian

Ingredients

  • 500g pork mince (from freezer — defrost first)
  • 250g orzo pasta
  • 2 tins chopped tomatoes (800g)
  • 1 bag frozen diced onions (~150g)
  • 4 frozen garlic cubes (or 3 cloves)
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée
  • 150g frozen peas
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 2 tbsp rapeseed oil
  • 300ml chicken stock (from frozen cubes)
  • 20g butter
  • Salt & black pepper

Prep Ahead

~20 min prep
Before you leave for pickup at 5:00 PM
Defrost pork mince — move from freezer to fridge this morning (or microwave defrost later) before
Defrost chicken stock cubes (300ml worth) — microwave or place in a jug of warm water before
Shape meatballs: mix mince with fennel seeds, half the oregano, a pinch of salt & pepper. Roll into ~20 walnut-sized balls. Place on a plate in the fridge before
Measure out frozen onions and garlic cubes into a small bowl. They'll part-defrost by cook time before
Open both tins of tomatoes. Measure orzo, peas, and tomato purée. Set everything by the hob before

Method

1
Brown the meatballs 8 mins
Heat rapeseed oil in a large deep pan or casserole over medium-high heat. Add the meatballs and brown on all sides — about 3–4 minutes, turning occasionally. They don't need to be cooked through. Remove to a plate.
2
Soften the onions 4 mins
In the same pan, add the frozen onions and garlic. Cook for 3–4 minutes over medium heat until softened and the water has cooked off. Add tomato purée and remaining oregano, stir for 30 seconds.
3
Build the sauce & add orzo 2 mins
Pour in the chopped tomatoes and chicken stock. Add honey and season with salt & pepper. Stir well. Bring to a simmer, then stir in the orzo pasta.
4
Simmer with meatballs 15 mins
Nestle the browned meatballs back into the sauce. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover with a lid, and simmer for 12 minutes, stirring every few minutes to prevent the orzo from sticking. The orzo absorbs the sauce as it cooks.
5
Add peas & finish 3 mins
Scatter in the frozen peas and stir through. Cook for a further 3 minutes uncovered until the peas are bright green and the orzo is tender with a rich, saucy consistency. Stir in butter to give it a glossy finish.
Consistency tip: The orzo thickens as it sits. If it looks too thick, splash in a little hot water or extra stock. You want it saucy, not stodgy.
6
Serve
Ladle into bowls. This is a one-pot dish — everything's in there. Great with a chunk of bread on the side if you have any.
Kid-friendly note: No heat or spice in this one. The fennel seeds give a gentle, slightly sweet flavour that kids tend to like. The meatballs and orzo are a foolproof combination — good finger food for the younger two if they want to pick at meatballs.
