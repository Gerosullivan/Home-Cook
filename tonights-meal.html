Family Beef Fajita Wraps

25 mins
Serves 5
Beef · Mexican

Ingredients

  • 500g beef mince (5–10% fat)
  • 500g Tesco Mexican-style veg mix (frozen)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée
  • Small pinch of salt
  • 8–10 soft tortilla wraps
  • 150–200g grated cheddar
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 150–200g sour cream or Greek yoghurt

Method

1
Brown the mince 5–6 mins
Heat a large pan over medium-high heat. Add mince (and oil if needed). Break it up well and cook for 5–6 minutes until fully browned. Tip off excess fat so it's not greasy for the kids.
2
Season 1 min
Add smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder and salt. Stir for 30 seconds to cook out the spices. Stir in the tomato purée — adds richness, not heat.
3
Cook the veg 6–8 mins
Tip the frozen veg mix straight into the pan. Cook for 6–8 minutes until hot and most of the moisture has cooked off. Let it sizzle a little at the end for better flavour — don't stew it.
Tip: Cook the veg mix until the steam is gone to avoid watery filling. Drain excess fat from the mince first.
4
Warm the wraps 2 mins
Warm wraps in a dry frying pan or microwave, just until soft and flexible. Keep wrapped in a tea towel so they stay warm.
5
Serve — build your own!
Serve everything in bowls on the table: wraps, beef & veg, cheese, sour cream, avocado — let the kids build their own. Optional: once filled, fold wraps and toast in a dry pan for 1–2 minutes per side, then cut in half for smaller hands.
6
Adults only
Put hot sauce, jalapeños, chilli flakes, or a squeeze of lime on the table separately for the grown-ups.
Kid-friendly note: Call them "cheesy beef wraps" instead of fajitas for better buy-in. All the heat stays off the pan and on the adult table.
