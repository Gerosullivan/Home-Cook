🥩 Cider-Glazed Pork Chops with Crushed Baby Potatoes & Buttered Carrots

~45 mins total
Serves 5
Pork · Irish
🍕 Friday note: Tonight's usually takeaway night — but if you fancy cooking, here's a lovely one using the pork chops from the freezer and the baby potatoes that need using up. Quick to prep, and the kids will love it.

Ingredients

  • 4 pork chops (from freezer — defrost in fridge from morning)
  • 1 bag baby potatoes (~500g), halved
  • 1 bag carrots (~400g), peeled & sliced into rounds
  • 200ml dry cider (or apple juice)
  • 2 tbsp wholegrain mustard
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 40g butter
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh thyme (a few sprigs)
  • Salt & pepper
  • 1 lemon (juice & zest)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional garnish)

Prep Ahead

~15 min prep
Before you leave for pickup at 5:00 PM — get the potatoes started and the glaze ready
Put baby potatoes into a pot of salted water. Bring to the boil, then reduce to a simmer. They'll par-boil while you prep the rest. before
Peel and slice carrots into rounds (~1cm thick). Set aside in a bowl. before
Make the glaze: whisk together cider, wholegrain mustard, honey, and minced garlic in a jug. Set aside. before
Take pork chops out of the fridge 15 mins before cooking. Season generously with salt & pepper on both sides. before
Drain par-boiled potatoes (they should be just fork-tender). Leave in the colander to steam dry. before
While you're at pickup
Pork chops come to room temperature — better sear. Potatoes steam dry in the colander — drier potatoes crush better.

Method

1
Sear the pork chops ~6 mins
Heat olive oil in a large skillet or heavy pan over medium-high heat. Sear pork chops for 3 minutes per side until golden brown. Remove and set aside on a plate.
🌡️ Thermometer reminder: Insert a wireless probe into the thickest chop before it goes back in the pan. Target: 63°C internal (it'll carry over to 65°C while resting). Pork is safe and juicy at this temp.
2
Deglaze with the cider glaze ~5 mins
In the same pan, pour in the cider-mustard-honey glaze. Scrape up all the browned bits from the pork. Add thyme sprigs. Let it bubble and reduce by about half — it'll thicken into a glossy sauce.
3
Return chops to the glaze ~8 mins
Nestle the pork chops back into the sauce. Reduce heat to medium-low. Spoon the glaze over the chops every couple of minutes. Cook until the probe reads 63°C. Remove chops and rest on a board, loosely tented with foil.
4
Crush the baby potatoes ~8 mins
While chops rest, heat 20g butter and a drizzle of oil in a separate pan. Add the par-boiled baby potatoes and press each one with a fork or the back of a spatula to flatten slightly. Fry for 3–4 mins each side until crispy and golden. Season with salt and a squeeze of lemon.
5
Butter the carrots ~8 mins (parallel with step 4)
Boil carrot rounds in salted water for 5–6 minutes until just tender. Drain. Toss with 20g butter, a pinch of salt, and a little lemon zest.
6
Plate up
Divide crushed potatoes and buttered carrots among plates. Slice the rested pork chops (optional — or serve whole). Spoon the reduced cider glaze over the chops. Scatter with fresh parsley if you have it.
👧 Kid-friendly note: This is naturally mild — the mustard mellows right out in the glaze and the honey sweetness appeals to kids. The crushed potatoes are a fun texture for small hands. Cut the pork into strips for the younger ones.
💡 Tip: If you don't have cider to hand, apple juice works grand — add a splash of cider vinegar for a bit of tang. Or just use chicken stock with a spoon of honey.
