Homemade Pizza

~40 mins active
Serves 5
Pork · Italian

Ingredients

  • 500g 00 flour (Tipo 00)
  • 10g fine sea salt
  • 7g sachet dried yeast
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • 325ml lukewarm water
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 400g tin plum tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (sauce)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 100g pepperoni slices
  • 150g ham, sliced or torn
  • 1 jar pickled red onion, drained
  • 150g mushrooms, sliced
  • 250g mozzarella, torn

Method

1
Make the dough 15 mins
Combine 500g 00 flour and 10g salt in a large bowl. Mix yeast and sugar into 325ml lukewarm water, let sit 5 minutes until frothy. Pour yeast water and olive oil into the flour. Mix with a fork, then knead by hand for 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
2
Rise the dough 1 hour
Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with cling film, and leave in a warm place for 1 hour until doubled in size. This is your pickup window — the dough does the work while you're gone.
3
Make the sauce 20 mins
Crush 2 cloves garlic and fry gently in olive oil for 1 minute. Add tinned tomatoes, oregano, salt and pepper. Simmer for 15–20 minutes, breaking up tomatoes. Blitz or mash until smooth.
Tip: Make the sauce while the dough is starting its rise — the timing works perfectly. Sauce improves as it sits.
4
Preheat the oven 5 mins before rolling
Preheat oven to 250°C (or as hot as it goes). If you have a pizza stone, put it in now — it needs to be screaming hot.
5
Roll and shape 5–8 mins
Punch down the risen dough and divide into 2–3 portions. Roll each out on a floured surface as thin as you can, using extra 00 flour to prevent sticking.
6
Assemble 5 mins
Transfer bases to floured baking trays or parchment. Spread sauce over each base, leaving a 1cm border. Top with torn mozzarella, pepperoni, ham, sliced mushrooms, and pickled red onion.
Kid-friendly note: Make one pizza plain cheese or just ham & cheese — the little ones can help place their own toppings. Pickled onion can be strong so keep it to one side or one pizza only.
7
Bake 10–12 mins per pizza
Bake for 10–12 minutes until the base is crisp and golden and the cheese is bubbling and charred in spots. You'll likely need to do 2 batches — keep the first warm in the turned-off oven while the second bakes.
8
Rest and serve
Rest for 2 minutes, then slice and serve. Let the kids pick their slices first — pepperoni is always a winner.
