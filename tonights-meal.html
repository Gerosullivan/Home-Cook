Family Beef Fajita Wraps

~25 mins total
Serves 5
Beef · Mexican

Ingredients

  • 500g beef mince (5–10% fat)
  • 500g Tesco Mexican-style veg mix (frozen)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée
  • Small pinch of salt
  • 8–10 soft tortilla wraps
  • 150–200g grated cheddar
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced or mashed
  • 150–200g sour cream or Greek yoghurt

Prep Ahead

~12 min prep
Before you leave for pickup at 5:00 PM
Measure the spice mix into a small bowl: smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder, pinch of salt. before
Grate 150–200g cheddar into a bowl, cover. before
Brown the mince: 5–6 mins in a large pan until fully cooked. Tip off excess fat. Stir in the spice mix and tomato purée for 30 seconds. Leave in the pan off the heat. before
Scoop sour cream/yoghurt into a serving bowl; set out the tortilla pack. before
While you're at pickup
Seasoned mince resting in the pan — just needs reheating and the veg added
Cheese grated and ready — covered to stop it drying out
Food safety: Wash the board, knife and hands after handling the raw mince. If the kitchen is warm, pop the cooked mince in the fridge until you're back. Leave the avocado until cook phase — a squeeze of lemon will stop it browning if you prep it early.

Method

1
Brown the mince 5–6 mins
Heat a large pan over medium-high heat. Add the mince (and a splash of oil if needed). Break it up well and cook until fully browned. Tip off excess fat so it's not greasy for the kids.
2
Add the spices 1 min
Stir in the paprika, cumin, garlic powder and salt for 30 seconds to cook out. Stir in the tomato purée for richness (no heat).
3
In with the veg 6–8 mins
Tip the frozen veg mix straight into the pan. Cook until hot and most of the moisture has cooked off. Let it sizzle at the end for better flavour — don't stew it.
4
Warm the wraps 2 mins
Warm the tortillas in a dry frying pan or microwave until soft and flexible. Wrap in a tea towel to keep warm.
5
Build-your-own on the table
Serve everything in bowls: wraps, beef & veg, cheese, sour cream, avocado. Let the kids build their own. Optional: fold filled wraps and toast in a dry pan 1–2 mins per side, then cut in half for smaller hands.
Kid-friendly note: This is mild as written. Put hot sauce, jalapeños, chilli flakes and a squeeze of lime on the table separately for the adults.
