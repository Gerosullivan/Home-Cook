Crispy-Skin Salmon with Lemon-Herb Crushed Potatoes & Buttered Peas

~35 mins total
Serves 5
Salmon

Ingredients

  • Salmon
  • 5 salmon fillets (skin-on, ~150g each)
  • 1 tbsp rapeseed oil
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Lemon-Herb Crushed Potatoes
  • 750g baby potatoes (unpeeled)
  • 30g butter
  • 1 lemon — zest and juice
  • A few sprigs fresh thyme, leaves picked
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • Buttered Peas
  • 300g frozen peas
  • 15g butter
  • Pinch of dried mint (optional)
  • Lemon Butter Sauce
  • 30g butter
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • 1 tsp dried parsley

Method

1
Boil the potatoes 18 mins
Put baby potatoes in a pot of cold salted water. Bring to the boil, then simmer for 15–18 minutes until a knife slides in easily. Drain and return to the pot.
2
Crush the potatoes 3 mins
Lightly crush the drained potatoes with a fork — you want them broken open but still chunky. Stir through butter, lemon zest and a squeeze of juice, thyme leaves, and sea salt. Cover and set aside.
3
Sear the salmon 7–8 mins
Heat rapeseed oil in a large non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Place salmon fillets skin-side down. Press gently with a fish slice for the first 30 seconds to stop curling. Cook for 4–5 minutes without moving until the skin is deeply golden and crisp. Flip and cook flesh-side for 2–3 minutes until just cooked through. Remove to a warm plate.
Tip: Don't move the salmon once it's skin-down. The key to crispy skin is patience — let the pan do the work. You'll see the flesh turning opaque from the bottom up; flip when it's about two-thirds cooked.
4
Cook the peas 3 mins
While the salmon cooks, boil frozen peas for 2–3 minutes. Drain, toss with butter and dried mint if using. Season with a pinch of salt.
5
Lemon butter sauce 1 min
In the same salmon pan (off the heat), add butter and let it melt in the residual heat. Squeeze in lemon juice and stir through dried parsley. Spoon over the salmon.
6
Plate up
Spoon crushed potatoes onto plates, lay a salmon fillet on top (skin-side up for maximum crunch), pile peas alongside, and drizzle the lemon butter over everything. Lemon wedges on the side.
Kid-friendly note: This is a really mild, buttery meal — no heat at all. If anyone doesn't fancy the lemon butter, plain butter and a squeeze of ketchup works grand. The crushed potatoes are always a hit.
Fishmonger note: Pick up 5 skin-on salmon fillets today. Irish spring salmon is in season — ask for centre-cut fillets if they have them (more even thickness, easier to cook).
