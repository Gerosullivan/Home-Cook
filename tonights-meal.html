🍳 Spanish Tortilla with Rocket & Avocado Salad

~45 mins total
Serves 5
Eggs · Spanish
Why this one? No meal planned for tonight and no fresh meat in the fridge. You've got plenty of eggs, the Maris Pipers and baby potatoes need using, and the rocket and avocado are on the turn — a classic Spanish tortilla pulls them all together. No spice, kid-friendly, and uses zero shopping.

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs
  • 600g potatoes (Maris Piper + baby), peeled & thinly sliced
  • 1 large white onion, thinly sliced
  • 200ml rapeseed or sunflower oil (for confit-frying)
  • Sea salt & black pepper
  • 1 bag fresh rocket
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • ½ lemon (juice)
  • 1 tbsp good olive or rapeseed oil (for salad)
  • Crusty bread or warmed tortilla wraps to serve

Prep Ahead

~25 min prep
Before you leave for pickup at 5:00 PM — get the potatoes & onions softened so it's a quick assembly later
Peel and thinly slice the potatoes (~3mm coins). Peel and thinly slice the onion. before
Heat the oil in a deep 24cm non-stick pan over medium-low heat. Add the potatoes and onions with a big pinch of salt. Cook gently 18–22 mins until totally soft (not browned), stirring now and then. before
Drain the potato/onion mix in a sieve set over a heatproof bowl — save the oil, you'll use a spoonful again. Spread the mix on a plate to cool a little. before
Crack the eggs into a large bowl, season well, beat lightly. Once the potatoes are warm (not hot), tip them in and stir to coat. Cover and leave on the counter. before
Wash the rocket and spin dry. Cover and pop in the fridge. before
While you're at pickup
The egg-and-potato mix sits and the eggs absorb starch from the spuds — this is the secret to a properly creamy tortilla. Don't skip the rest.
🧼 Food safety: Wash the chopping board and knife after the onions/potatoes if they touched anything else. The egg mix is fine on the counter for 30–40 mins; if your kitchen is warm, slide the bowl into the fridge before you leave.

Method

1
Heat the pan ~2 mins
Wipe out the non-stick pan. Add 1 tbsp of the reserved oil and place over medium heat.
2
Cook the tortilla — first side ~6–7 mins
Give the egg mix a good stir and pour it in. Use a spatula to nudge the edges in and let raw egg run underneath. Lower the heat to medium-low and let it set on the bottom — you want the top still a bit wobbly.
3
The flip ~30 secs
Slide the tortilla onto a flat plate the size of the pan. Cover with a second plate, flip the lot, then slide back into the pan, raw side down.
💡 Tip: If the flip feels intimidating, finish it under a hot grill for 2–3 mins instead until just set on top. No shame in that.
4
Cook the second side ~3–4 mins
Tuck the edges in with the spatula to give it a neat curved shape. Cook 3–4 mins more — the centre should still feel a touch soft when you press it. Slide onto a board and let it rest for 5 mins.
5
Dress the salad ~2 mins (during rest)
Tip the rocket into a bowl. Lay the sliced avocado over the top, squeeze on the lemon (stops it browning), drizzle with the good oil, season with salt and pepper, toss lightly.
6
Slice and serve
Cut the tortilla into wedges. Serve with the rocket-and-avocado salad and warmed tortilla wraps or crusty bread.
👧 Kid-friendly note: The two littles will likely prefer the tortilla on its own — cut into small fingers, it's basically a posh omelette and goes down a treat. Skip the rocket for them and offer plain avocado slices on the side instead.
