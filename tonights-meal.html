🍗 Gochujang Roast Chicken with Spring Greens

~1 hr 30 mins total
Serves 5
Chicken · Korean

Ingredients

  • 1.5kg whole chicken
  • 1 tbsp gochujang
  • 1 lemon, juiced (halves reserved)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable or sunflower oil
  • 2 onions, each cut into 8 wedges
  • A few lemon thyme or oregano sprigs
  • 250ml chicken stock
  • 500g spring greens, shredded
  • 250g purple sprouting broccoli
  • 1 tbsp wholegrain mustard

Method

1
Glaze & roast the chicken at high heat before pickup — 20 mins at 220°C
Heat the oven to 220°C/200°C fan/gas 7. Combine the gochujang, half the lemon juice, the oil, salt and pepper, and rub all over the chicken. Tuck the lemon halves into the cavity along with one of the onions and half the herbs. Pour stock into the base of the tin, scatter in the remaining onion and herbs, and roast for 20 minutes.
🌡️ Thermometer reminder: Insert the wireless probe into the thickest part of the thigh, avoiding bone. Target 68°C internal temperature. The thermometer will alert you — no guessing.
⚠️ Wash up: Wash your hands, board, and anything that touched raw chicken thoroughly before you leave.
2
Reduce heat & continue roasting 30 mins at 180°C
Reduce the oven to 180°C/160°C fan/gas 4 and cook for a further 30 minutes. The glaze should be turning sticky and dark.
3
Add the greens & broccoli 20 mins
Lift the chicken out of the tin. Mix the spring greens and purple sprouting broccoli through the stock and onions in the tin. Sit the chicken back on top and return to the oven for a further 20 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and the veg is tender.
4
Finish & serve
Remove from the oven. Stir the wholegrain mustard and remaining lemon juice through the greens. Carve the chicken and serve with the mustardy greens and broccoli spooned alongside.
👧 Kid-friendly note: Gochujang has a mild-to-medium heat. For the kids, carve their portions from the breast where less glaze collects. Wipe off excess glaze before plating, or serve their chicken pieces with a little butter instead. The mustardy greens are also punchy — serve the kids' greens before stirring in the mustard.
Leftovers: Strip any remaining chicken off the bone tonight. The gochujang flavour is brilliant in a wrap with leftover greens for tomorrow's lunch. The carcass makes a good stock with the roasted onion and lemon in the cavity.
