The Best Spaghetti Bolognese

~1 hr 50 mins total
Serves 5
Beef · Italian

Ingredients

  • Main
  • 500g fresh beef mince
  • 400g dried spaghetti
  • 4 rashers smoked streaky bacon, finely diced
  • 2 tins plum/chopped tomatoes
  • 6 fresh cherry tomatoes
  • 2 medium onions, finely diced
  • 2 sticks celery, finely diced
  • 2 carrots, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely diced
  • 75g Parmesan, freshly grated (plus extra)
  • 2 tbsp tomato purée
  • 1 beef stock cube
  • Herbs & Seasoning
  • 2–3 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • Handful of fresh basil (plus extra)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1–2 fresh bay leaves
  • 1 medium chilli, seeded & sliced (optional)
  • Sea salt and black pepper
  • Olive oil
  • Optional / To Serve
  • Glass of red wine (optional)
  • Crusty bread, to serve

Method

1
Prep the veg and bacon 15 mins
Finely dice the onions, carrots, celery, and garlic. Finely dice the bacon. Halve the cherry tomatoes. Strip the leaves from the rosemary sprigs and discard the stalks.
2
Fry the bacon 5 mins
Place a large heavy-bottomed saucepan on a medium heat. Add a good lug of olive oil and gently fry the bacon until golden and crisp.
3
Cook the soffritto 8–10 mins
Reduce the heat slightly and add the onions, carrots, celery, garlic, and rosemary leaves. Stir everything around and fry for 8–10 minutes until the veg has softened.
4
Brown the mince 5 mins
Increase the heat slightly and add the beef mince. Stir until the meat is browned all over.
5
Build the sauce 3 mins
Stir in the tinned tomatoes (plum are best — less water). Add tomato purée, stock cube, oregano, bay leaves, basil, chilli (if using), and the wine if using. Halve the cherry tomatoes and throw them in. Break up the plum tomatoes with a wooden spoon.
6
Long simmer 1 hr 15 mins
Bring to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat to low–medium. Put the lid on and leave it blipping away for about 1 hour 15 minutes. Stir occasionally to make sure it doesn’t catch. The sauce should develop into a wonderfully rich, tomatoey ragù.
7
Finish and cook spaghetti 10–12 mins
When the sauce is nearly ready, stir in the Parmesan and season with salt and pepper to taste. Meanwhile, cook the spaghetti in a large pan of salted boiling water according to packet instructions.
8
Combine and serve
Drain the spaghetti in a colander and add it to the pan with the sauce. Give it all a good stir, coating the pasta in the lovely tomato sauce. Serve with a little grated Parmesan, fresh basil leaves, and crusty bread on the side.
Kid-friendly note: Skip the chilli entirely for a mild, crowd-pleasing sauce the kids will love. The long simmer makes it lovely and sweet. Serve extra Parmesan on the side for sprinkling.
