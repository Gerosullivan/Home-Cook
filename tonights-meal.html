Honey-Garlic Pork Chop Traybake

~15 min prep + 30 min oven
Serves 5
Pork · Home Comfort
Saturday Chef's Pick: It's normally takeaway night, but you've 4 pork chops in the freezer calling your name. Dead easy traybake — everything on one tray, oven does the work. Grand if you'd rather order in though!

Ingredients

  • 4 pork chops (from freezer — defrost in fridge from morning)
  • 1 bag frozen broccoli florets (~350g)
  • 1 bag frozen sliced carrots (~300g)
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced (or 1 tsp garlic powder)
  • 2 tbsp rapeseed oil
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds (to finish)

Method

1
Defrost the chops morning job
Move the 4 pork chops from the freezer to the fridge first thing in the morning. They'll be fully defrosted by evening.
2
Make the glaze 3 mins
In a small bowl, whisk together 2 tbsp honey, 2 tbsp light soy sauce, minced garlic, 1 tsp paprika, and 1 tbsp rapeseed oil. Set half aside for basting later.
3
Prep the tray 5 mins
Spread the frozen broccoli and carrots across a large roasting tray. Drizzle with 1 tbsp rapeseed oil and season with pepper. Pat chops dry, season with pepper, coat with the first half of the glaze, and nestle them on top of the veg.
4
Preheat the oven after pickup
Preheat oven to 200°C fan (220°C conventional). Take the tray out of the fridge while the oven heats — 5 minutes at room temp is fine.
5
Roast 25–30 mins
Roast for 15 minutes, then pull the tray out and brush the reserved glaze over the chops. Return to the oven for another 10–15 minutes until the pork is cooked through and the edges are sticky and caramelised.
Thermometer reminder: Probe the thickest part of a chop — you're looking for 68°C internal temp. No guessing with the wireless thermometers!
6
Rest and serve 5 mins
Rest the chops on the tray for 5 minutes (the juices redistribute and the glaze gets even stickier). Sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve straight from the tray.
Kid-friendly note: The glaze is sweet and mild — the little ones will love it. Cut the youngest two's portions into strips. One chop between the two of them is plenty.
