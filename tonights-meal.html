🍖 Pork & Herb Meatballs with Buttery Mash & Onion Gravy

~50 mins total
Serves 5
Pork · Comfort / Irish
🍂 Sunday note: A proper Sunday dinner using the pork mince from the freezer. Hearty meatballs, creamy mash, and a rich onion gravy — the kids will love rolling these into balls with you. No spice, all comfort.

Ingredients

  • 500g pork mince (from freezer — defrost in fridge from morning)
  • 50g fresh breadcrumbs (2 slices of bread, blitzed)
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves (stripped from sprigs)
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely grated
  • Salt & pepper
  • 2 tbsp rapeseed oil
  • 1kg Maris Piper potatoes (or baby potatoes), peeled & quartered
  • 60g butter
  • 100ml milk, warmed
  • 2 red onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp plain flour
  • 400ml chicken stock (from freezer)
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp honey

Prep Ahead

~20 min prep
Before you leave for pickup at 5:00 PM — get the mash started, meatballs shaped, and gravy onions cooking
Peel and quarter potatoes. Put into a large pot of salted cold water. Bring to the boil, then simmer for 15–20 mins until fork-tender. before
Thinly slice red onions. Start them in a pan with 20g butter and a pinch of salt over low heat. They'll slowly caramelise while you prep. before
Make the meatballs: combine pork mince, breadcrumbs, beaten egg, thyme, parsley, garlic, and seasoning. Mix gently — don't overwork. Roll into golf-ball sized meatballs (you should get about 15–18). Place on a plate. before
Give the caramelising onions a stir. They should be softening and turning golden. Turn heat to lowest setting before you leave. before
Drain the cooked potatoes. Leave in the pot with the lid off to steam dry for a minute. Turn off heat. before
While you're at pickup
Onions continue to slowly caramelise on lowest heat — they'll be beautifully sweet and jammy by the time you're back. Potatoes steam dry in the pot — drier potatoes make fluffier mash.

Method

1
Brown the meatballs ~8 mins
Heat rapeseed oil in a large non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the meatballs and brown on all sides, turning gently every couple of minutes. They don't need to be cooked through — just a good golden crust. Remove to a plate.
2
Make the mash ~5 mins (parallel with step 1)
While the meatballs brown, mash the drained potatoes with 40g butter and the warm milk. Season well with salt and pepper. Beat until smooth and creamy. Cover and set aside — it'll stay warm.
3
Build the onion gravy ~5 mins
Check your caramelised onions — they should be deep golden and soft. Stir in plain flour and cook for 1 minute. Gradually pour in the chicken stock, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Add the Worcestershire sauce and honey. Bring to a gentle simmer.
4
Simmer meatballs in the gravy ~10 mins
Nestle the browned meatballs into the onion gravy. Reduce heat to medium-low and let them simmer gently, turning once, until cooked through — about 10 minutes. The gravy will thicken as it goes.
5
Plate up
Spoon generous helpings of buttery mash onto each plate. Top with meatballs and ladle the onion gravy over everything. Serve with some steamed greens on the side if you have them.
👧 Kid-friendly note: These meatballs are naturally mild — no spice at all. The sweet onion gravy is a hit with kids. For the younger ones, cut each meatball in half so they cool faster. Great for little hands to pick up too.
💡 Tip: If you want to stretch this further, toss in a handful of frozen peas to the gravy in the last 3 minutes. They add colour and the kids will eat them in the gravy without complaint.
💡 Leftover mince: You've 500g pork mince left in the freezer — save it for another day. It makes great pork burgers or a stir-fry base.
