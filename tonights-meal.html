Chicken Chasseur (Ger's Version)

~55 mins total
Serves 5
Chicken · French

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (~1.2kg), broken down into thighs and breasts
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • 300g shallots, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, roughly chopped
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 300g closed cup mushrooms, quartered
  • 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 200ml white wine (or red)
  • 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • Fresh tarragon, to finish (or 1 tsp dried)

Method

1
Break down the chicken 5 mins
Remove thighs and breasts (bone-in, skin-on) from the whole chicken. Set aside drumsticks and wings for stock another day.
2
Season the chicken 1 min
Season all chicken pieces generously with salt and pepper.
3
Brown the chicken 8–10 mins
Heat olive oil in a large heavy pan or casserole over medium-high heat. Brown chicken skin-side down for 8–10 minutes, turning once, until golden all over. Work in batches if needed. Remove and set aside.
Food safety: Wash your board and knife after handling the raw chicken. Clean up any splashes from browning.
4
Soften the aromatics 2 mins
Add sliced shallots and garlic to the pan. Cook for 1 minute, scraping up any fond. Stir in the flour and cook for 1 minute, mixing well.
5
Build the sauce 2 mins
Add quartered mushrooms, tinned tomatoes, wine, thyme, and soy sauce. Stir to combine.
6
Braise the chicken 35–40 mins
Return chicken to the pan, nestling pieces into the sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook for 35–40 minutes until chicken is cooked through.
Thermometer check: Chicken should reach 68°C internal. Probe the thickest part of the breast — pop a wireless thermometer in before you leave for pickup and you'll know it's done when you're back.
7
Check and finish 2 mins
Check the chicken is cooked through (68°C). Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning. Sprinkle with fresh tarragon.
8
Serve
Serve with mashed potato. The sauce is rich and tomatoey — perfect for soaking up with the mash.
Kid-friendly note: This dish is mild and saucy — kids tend to love it. The mushrooms can be fished out for any fussy eaters. Serve with extra mash on the side.
