🥩 Steak with Buttery Mash, Fried Onions & Veg

~40 mins total
Serves 5
Beef · Classic

Ingredients

  • 2 large sirloin or striploin steaks (~300g each)
  • 1kg potatoes (Maris Piper or Rooster), peeled & quartered
  • 1 bag frozen vegetables (peas, sweetcorn & carrots work well)
  • 1 large onion, sliced into rings
  • 60g butter (40g for mash, 20g for onions)
  • 80ml milk, warmed
  • 1 tbsp rapeseed or sunflower oil
  • Salt & freshly ground black pepper

Prep Ahead

~25 min prep
Before you leave for pickup at 5:00 PM — get the potatoes cooked and steaks to room temp
Take steaks out of the fridge. Leave on a plate on the counter to come to room temperature. Pat dry with kitchen paper. before
Peel and quarter potatoes. Put into a large pot of salted cold water. Bring to the boil, then simmer for 15–20 mins until fork-tender. before
Peel and slice onion into rings. Set aside. before
Season steaks generously with salt and pepper on both sides. before
Drain cooked potatoes. Leave in the pot with the lid off to steam dry for a minute. Turn off heat. before
While you're at pickup
Steaks continue coming to room temperature — this ensures an even cook and better sear. Potatoes steam dry in the pot — drier potatoes make fluffier mash.

Method

1
Make the mash ~5 mins
Mash the drained potatoes with 40g butter and the warm milk. Season well with salt and pepper. Beat until smooth and creamy. Cover and set aside — it stays warm.
2
Fry the onions ~8 mins
Melt 20g butter in a frying pan over medium heat. Add the sliced onion rings and cook, stirring occasionally, until soft and golden brown. Season with a pinch of salt. Remove to a bowl and keep warm.
3
Cook the steaks ~6–8 mins
Get the same pan smoking hot. Add a splash of oil. Lay the steaks in and cook without moving for 3–4 minutes until a deep golden crust forms. Flip and cook the other side for 3–4 minutes for medium. Add a knob of butter to the pan in the last minute and baste the steaks.
🌡️ Thermometer: Probe your steaks — pull at 55–58°C for medium. They'll carry over a few degrees while resting. Use one of your wireless thermometers to get it right.
4
Rest the steaks ~5 mins
Transfer steaks to a warm plate and tent loosely with foil. Resting lets the juices redistribute — don't skip this step. Meanwhile, cook the veg.
5
Heat the veg ~4 mins (during rest)
Cook the frozen veg according to packet instructions — microwave or a quick boil. Drain and add a small knob of butter.
6
Slice and serve
Slice each steak against the grain into strips. Serve on plates with a generous scoop of buttery mash, a pile of fried onions on top, and the veg alongside. Pour any resting juices from the plate over the steak.
👧 Kid-friendly note: Slice the kids' steak into small, thin strips so it cools quickly and is easy to eat. The fried onions and buttery mash are always a hit. If the younger two are fussy about veg, mix the peas into the mash — they'll never know.
💡 Tip: Two large steaks between five is about right — give the adults larger portions and cut smaller strips for the three kids. If you fancy a sauce, deglaze the steak pan with a splash of stock and a teaspoon of mustard for a quick pan sauce.
