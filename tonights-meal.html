🍜 Authentic Pad Thai (Prawn)

~40 mins total
Serves 5
Prawns · Thai

Ingredients

Scaled to 5 servings (original serves 2). Cook in 2–3 batches — a wok can only handle 2 portions at a time.

  • 90g palm sugar, chopped
  • 120ml water (for sauce)
  • 8 tbsp Thai tamarind paste
  • 5 tbsp fish sauce
  • 290g dry rice noodles (medium), soaked 1 hr
  • 5 tbsp dried shrimp, roughly chopped
  • 8 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 150g shallots, roughly chopped
  • 210g pressed tofu, small pieces
  • 8 tbsp sweet preserved daikon radish
  • Dried chilli flakes (adults only)
  • 8 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 25 medium prawns
  • 5 eggs
  • 300g bean sprouts
  • 175g garlic chives, cut into 5cm pieces
  • 60g roasted peanuts, roughly chopped
  • 3 limes, cut into wedges

Method

This recipe is cooked in 2–3 batches. A wok only does 2 portions properly — overcrowding steams instead of fries. Each batch takes ~5 minutes. Keep finished batches warm in a low oven (100°C).

1
Make the sauce ~5 mins (done in prep)
Melt palm sugar in a small pot over medium heat. Stir until it darkens in colour. Immediately add water, fish sauce, and tamarind paste. The sugar will harden — this is fine. Bring to a simmer, then turn off heat. Let it sit while you prep; the sugar will dissolve by the time you need it.
2
Prep noodles & aromatics done in prep
Cut drained noodles once with scissors so they're half as long (easier to toss). In a bowl, combine tofu, garlic, shallots, preserved radish, dried shrimp, and chilli flakes.
3
Sear the prawns ~3 mins per batch
Heat a wok or large non-stick skillet over high heat. Add enough oil to coat the bottom. Sear 8–9 prawns until just done, then remove. Repeat for remaining prawns. Set all aside.
4
Fry the aromatics ~2 mins per batch
In the same wok over medium heat, add a little more oil. Add one batch of the tofu/garlic/shallot mixture. Sauté for a few minutes until garlic starts to turn golden and shallots wilt. Don't skimp on oil or the noodles will clump.
5
Add noodles & sauce ~2 mins per batch
Turn heat to high, add one batch of noodles and sauce. Toss until all the sauce is absorbed. If noodles are still undercooked, add a splash of water and keep cooking.
6
Add the eggs ~1 min per batch
Push noodles to one side. Add a little oil to the empty space, crack in 1–2 eggs, break the yolks. Put noodles on top of the eggs and cook for about 30 seconds. Flip and toss to mix eggs through.
7
Finish each batch ~1 min
Add that batch's prawns back in plus any juices, then bean sprouts, garlic chives, and some peanuts. Turn off heat and toss until well mixed. Transfer to serving dish (or keep warm in low oven). Repeat steps 4–7 for remaining batches.
8
Serve
Serve immediately with lime wedges and extra peanuts on top. Squeeze lime over before eating.
👧 Kid-friendly note: Pad Thai shouldn't be spicy for the kids — just leave the dried chilli flakes out of their batches entirely. The palm sugar sauce is sweet and tangy which kids usually love. If the fish sauce smell bothers them, don't worry — it mellows completely when cooked.
💡 Wok tip: The secret to good pad thai is a screaming hot wok and not overcrowding it. Two portions per batch max. If the noodles start clumping, add a splash of water and a bit more oil.
🥜 Peanut note: Keep chopped peanuts on the side for topping — easier for the kids to skip if they prefer.
